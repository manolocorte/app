NIHPO's Synthetic Health Data Platform generates highly realistic, scientifically-correct and very customized synthetic health data for use in clinical trials.

The synthetic health data includes:

  • synthetic patients with synthetic Personal Health Records (PHR)
  • synthetic clinical trial result data, in customer-specific formats, including CDISC SDTM

NIHPO-generated synthetic health data helps life sciences companies to reduce the time it takes to design, develop, test, validate, and obtain regulatory approval for new drugs and medical devices.

This work is guided by a close collaboration with executives at pharma companies:
https://www.pharmiweb.com/press-release/2020-07-06/phuse-deploys-nihpo-platform-of-enriched-open-data-in-microsoft-azure-to-accelerate-covid-19-clinical-research

This paper describes the NIHPO Synthetic Health Data Platform in detail:
http://nihpo.com/AD-162.mp4
http://nihpo.com/AD-162_PharmaSUG2021_12.pdf

The platform currently offers 02 functional blocks:

Synth PHR

In this page you can generate a full Synthetic Personal Health Record ("SynthPHR") for individuals based on your exact needs.

You can define:

  • Country of the world where synthetic persons are located in.
  • Gender distribution: percentages of Female and Male individuals.
  • Age range: between 1 and 100 years of age.
  • Real World Data: add drugs from either the FDA or the EMA; and medical devices from the FDA.
  • Output format: select either JSON or SQLite3.

Once your SynthPHR file is ready you can then proceed to the next step, creating a Synthetic Clinical Trial ("SynthTrial").

Synth Trial

In this page you can generate the output of a synthetic clinical trial.

You can define:

  • Trial summary parameters: all the parameters that create the trial design structure.
  • Variables not controlled by standards: list of possible values for each variable.
  • Arms, Elements and Visits: build the number of arms, elements and visits needed in the specific trial.
  • Output format: select either SQLite3, csv or SAS XPORT.

The output files use the CDISC SDTM format.
